Multimodal Biometric Authentication Project

[cite_start]

Fusion of face and palmprint biometrics using Python, OpenCV, and scikit-learn to enhance authentication security and accuracy, especially when face coverings are present[cite: 1, 9]. [cite_start]This system demonstrates improved robustness over single-modality methods by combining the unique features of both traits[cite: 8].

Project Overview
[cite_start]

Traditional single-modality biometric systems are vulnerable to spoofing and fail when the biometric input is obscured, a problem highlighted during the COVID-19 pandemic with face masks[cite: 6, 13, 146]. [cite_start]This project addresses these issues by fusing facial and palmprint data at the score-level to create a more resilient and accurate authentication system[cite: 7, 66].

Key Research Questions
    [cite_start]
  • How does combining face and palmprint data affect authentication performance compared to a single modality? [cite: 19]
    • [cite_start]
  • What is the impact of facial occlusions (like masks and glasses) on the multimodal system's performance? [cite: 21]
Methods & Datasets

The system was developed using Python, Dlib, and scikit-learn. [cite_start]It leverages two public datasets: the Specs on Faces (SoF) Dataset for facial images with occlusions and the Birjand University Mobile Palmprint Database (BMPD)[cite: 17, 46]. [cite_start]Features were extracted from each and fed into five classification models (KNN, Decision Tree, SVM, Logistic Regression, LDA) [cite: 50-52, 56-61].

Key Results
[cite_start]

The k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) classifier performed best for both individual modalities, with an Equal Error Rate (EER) of 0.192 for faces and 0.196 for palmprints[cite: 79, 81]. [cite_start]While the face modality showed better separation between genuine and impostor scores, the fusion of both created a more balanced and robust system, validating the hypothesis that multimodal systems are more resilient, especially against occlusions[cite: 85, 87, 93, 97].
